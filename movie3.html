The Thing (1982)

Synopsis

In the isolated chill of an Antarctic research station, a group of American scientists are disrupted by a frantic helicopter chase. The Norwegians from a nearby base are hot on the heels of a seemingly ordinary sled dog, firing with deadly intent. When the dog takes refuge with the Americans, things take a horrifying turn. The creature they take in is no ordinary canine – it's a shape-shifting alien that can perfectly imitate any living being it consumes.

Kurt Russell portrays R.J. MacReady, the station's helicopter pilot, who finds himself leading the team in a desperate fight for survival. As paranoia sets in, the researchers can't trust anyone, not even each other. Any seemingly normal colleague could be a horrifying imposter. The Thing picks them off one by one, using their own bodies against them. MacReady, with the help of the station's doctor, must find a way to unmask the alien before it's too late, all while battling the harsh wintery environment and the present fear of who – or what – is standing next to them.

Review

The Thing (1982) is for sure mind-blowing. This movie has kept me thinking about it for the past week.

The effects are 10/10. There isn’t any CGI; all the effects are practical. Gnarly transformations, gooey bits flying everywhere – it's enough to make your stomach churn. They don't hold back on the gore, which makes the movie a little gross to watch.

The characters might not be winning any Academy Awards, but they're just a bunch of scientists stuck in a frozen wasteland being stalked by an alien. Kurt Russell is awesome though. He plays MacReady, the chopper pilot.

Is it perfect?Nah, some of the guys do dumb stuff sometimes, and the ending is kinda underwhelming. Me personally, I think all of the movie is perfectly done. As I wrote, there's no need to seriously develop the characters. The Thing might leave you feeling sick due to the disturbing effects, however, you should for sure do yourself a favor and check it out!
