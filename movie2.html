Arrival (2016)

Synopsis

Arrival tells the story of Louise Banks, a linguistics expert recruited by the military to decipher the language of alien beings who have arrived on Earth in mysterious spacecraft. As tensions rise between nations, Louise must race against time to understand the aliens' message before war erupts. The film explores communication and language's impact on "perception of time itself."

Review

Arrival (2016) isn't your typical alien invasion movie. While there aren’t any giant spaceships blasting laser beams, this movie is a mind-bender that will have you thinking about it for weeks or months.

Amy Adams knocks it out of the park as Louise Banks, a linguistics professor who gets pulled into a top-secret mission to communicate with aliens who've landed in giant, mysterious pods around the world. There's no easy way to translate their language, like google translate. Louise is basically humanity's last hope for understanding these visitors and preventing a global meltdown.

The film is slow burn, but in the best way possible. Director Denis Villeneuve weaves a beautiful and suspenseful story, building tension not with explosions but with the unknown. The alien language itself is fascinating, all squiggles and circles that look like they belong on a toddler's drawing. But Louise, with her sharp mind and even sharper determination, starts to try to crack the code.

Is it a perfect movie? Maybe not for everyone. It’s nothing like the other movies I wrote about, so if you're looking for nonstop action and flashy aliens, this might not be your cup of tea. But if you're up for an intelligent and thought-provoking sci-fi experience, Arrival is a must-watch.
